# Summary: White Sox Manager Will Venable - Second Season Profile

## Key Facts

### Background
- **Second season** as White Sox manager
- Team set MLB record with **121 losses** in 2023
- **2025 record:** 60-102 (net gain of 19 wins from 2023)

### 2026 Team Status
- **Payroll:** 28th-highest in MLB at **$101.5M**
- **No locked-in bullpen or rotation** - **9 pitchers competing** for starting spots
- **Season opener:** March 26 vs. Milwaukee Brewers (2025 AL champions)
- Lost **5 of 6 games** to start season

### Venable's Baseball Pedigree
- All-Ivy League at Princeton (baseball/basketball)
- Son of MLB player Max Venable
- 9-year MLB career as outfielder (mostly with Padres)
- Coaching stints under **Bruce Bochy, Joe Maddon, and Alex Cora**

### Key Players
- **Miguel Vargas (OF):** Grand slam in team's first win - promising sign
- **Munetaka Murakami (OF):** Bright spots but defensive scrutiny

## Notable Quotes

- *"Oh, I learned stuff every single day."* — Will Venable on managing the White Sox
- *"Honestly, losing is not fun. But it didn't take a personal toll on me. I signed up for everything required to ensure these guys come to the ballpark prepared to play every day regardless of the previous day's results."*
- *"I had the energy for it. I was positive about it and excited to do it every day."*
- *"There's been tons of help from baseball ops. I have a great partnership with that group and they've been very supportive."*
- *"It's a collaborative process."* (on determining Opening Day starter)
