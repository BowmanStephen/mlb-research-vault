# **Dylan Cease Sets Blue Jays Record with 12 Strikeouts in Debut Win**
**Toronto Blue Jays** | **March 29, 2026** | *Yahoo Sports*
**Author:** Ian Casselberry

---

## **📌 Key Highlights & Records**
### **🔥 Cease's Historic Debut**
- **12 strikeouts** in **5⅓ innings** – **Blue Jays record** for a pitching debut.
- **Tied team record** with **7 consecutive strikeouts** (matching Alex Manoah, 2021).
- **1 run allowed**, **3 hits** in a **walk-off 8-7, 11-inning win** over the **Oakland Athletics**.

> *"Cease demonstrated why he received a **$210 million contract** in free agency."*

### **Comparison to Previous Record**
- **David Price** held the previous Blue Jays debut strikeout record (**11 Ks**, 2015).
- Price went **9-1 with a 2.10 ERA** in 11 starts after being traded from the Tigers.

---

## **📊 Game Summary: Blue Jays 8, Athletics 7 (11 innings)**

### **📍 Rogers Centre, Toronto**
**Pitcher:** Dylan Cease (5⅓ IP, 12 K, 1 ER, 3 H)
**Win:** **Ernie Clement** (walk-off RBI single in 11th)

### **📈 Key Moments**
- **Cease's dominance (1st-5th innings):**
  - **12 strikeouts**, **1 run allowed** (1st inning).
  - **Walk-off hero:** **Ernie Clement** scores **Nathan Lukes** with a **fastball down the middle** in the 11th.

- **Cease's exit (6th inning):**
  - **90 pitches** by the 6th inning.
  - **Walk to Nick Kurtz**, **RBI double to Tyler Soderstrom** (1-1).
  - **Brent Rooker** reaches on a fielder's choice → **Braydon Fisher** relieves Cease.

- **Athletics rally (7th inning):**
  - **5 runs** (including a **Shea Langeliers grand slam** off Brendon Little).
  - **Toronto chases back:** **Vladimir Guerrero Jr. (RBI single)**, **Jesús Sánchez & Andrés Giménez (singles)**.

- **Walk-off (11th inning):**
  - **Athletics load bases** (2 outs, runners on 2nd & 3rd).
  - **Intentional walk to Daulton Varsho** → **Ernie Clement's RBI single** wins it.

### **🎯 Blue Jays' Offensive Struggles**

### **📉 Cease's Durability & Pitch Count**
- **90 pitches in 5⅓ innings** → **High workload** (relieved at 6th inning).
