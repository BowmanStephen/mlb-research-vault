# White Sox Manager Will Venable: Lessons Learned in His Second Season
*Comprehensive Summary*

---

## **Key Excerpts**
> *"Oh, I learned stuff every single day."*
> — **Will Venable**, on the relentless learning curve of managing the White Sox.

> *"Honestly, losing is not fun. But it didn't take a personal toll on me. I signed up for everything required to ensure these guys come to the ballpark prepared to play every day regardless of the previous day's results."*

> *"I had the energy for it. I was positive about it and excited to do it every day."*

> *"There's been tons of help from baseball ops. I have a great partnership with that group and they've been very supportive."*

> *"It's a collaborative process."* (on determining the Opening Day starter)

---

## **Background & Context**
- **Will Venable** is in his **second season** as manager of the **Chicago White Sox**, a team still rebuilding after setting a **MLB record with 121 losses** in 2023.
- The team improved slightly in 2025, finishing with a **60-102 record** (a net gain of **19 wins**).
- Venable's **2026 season** begins with **high expectations** but **realistic challenges**, including:
  - **28th-highest payroll** ($101.5M) in MLB.
  - **No formalized bullpen or rotation** yet, with **nine pitchers vying for starting spots**.
  - **Opening Day starter** still undecided.

---

## **Venable's Approach & Mindset**

### **Embracing the Grind**
- Venable **rejected negativity** about the team's struggles, focusing instead on **daily preparation**.
- He emphasizes **consistent effort** and **positive energy**, even in a losing environment.

### **Learning Curve & Adaptability**
- Venable admits he **learns something new daily**, citing:
  - **Player deployment strategies**
  - **Communication techniques**
  - **Practice structuring**
  - **Collaboration with baseball operations**

### **Mentorship & Pedigree**
- Venable's **baseball pedigree**:
  - **All-Ivy League** at Princeton (baseball/basketball).
  - **Son of MLB player Max Venable**.
  - **9-year MLB career** as an outfielder (mostly with the **Padres**).
  - **Coaching stints** under legends like **Bruce Bochy, Joe Maddon, and Alex Cora**.
- He draws from **multiple winning systems**.

---

## **Team & Season Outlook**

### **Current Status (Mid-Spring Training, March 2026)**
- **No bullpen or rotation locked in**—**9 pitchers competing for spots**.
- **Season opener**: **March 26 vs. Milwaukee Brewers** (2025 AL champions).
- **Early-season struggles**: Lost **5 of 6 games** to start.

### **Key Players & Roles**
- **Miguel Vargas** (OF) has shown **promise** with a **grand slam** in the team's first win.
- **Munetaka Murakami** (OF) has provided **bright spots** but faces **defensive scrutiny**.

---

## **Actionable Takeaways**

### **For the White Sox:**
- **Bullpen/rotation finalization** is critical—**9 pitchers competing** creates uncertainty.
- **Defensive improvements** are needed.
- **Player development** must balance **high expectations** with **realistic growth**.

### **For Venable:**
- **Leverage his coaching network** (Bochy, Maddon, Cora) for strategic insights.
- **Double down on baseball ops collaboration**.
- **Focus on small wins** to build momentum.
