# **The Colorado Rockies: How They Became One of MLB's Worst Teams in History (2025 Season)**

---
## **Key Excerpts & Critical Data**
### **Records & Performance**
- **2025 Season**: **98 losses** (3rd straight 100-loss season).
  - **Modern MLB record**: 121 losses (set by Chicago White Sox in 2024).
  - **Rankings**:
    - **29th in runs scored**
    - **30th in runs allowed**
    - **Worst run differential (-412)** in modern MLB history.
- **Attendance**: Dropped from **3M+ fans (2018, playoff years)** to **declining support** due to poor performance.
  - **2019**: 6th in MLB attendance.
  - **2025**: 15th in MLB attendance.
  - **Fan sentiment**: ~75% of fans at Coors Field root for opponents (e.g., Cubs) in some games.

### **Player & Organizational Quotes**
> *"It has gotten to a point ... where it's something I don't enjoy seeing in my home ballpark. Inside of our own ballpark, when we have 60/40, sometimes 70/30 split of fans from the other side, it eats at you a little bit."*
> — **Kyle Freeland**, Rockies pitcher & Denver native.

> *"I think you have to look in the mirror at three 100-loss seasons in a row ... and I think you have to look at what kind of changes can be made."*
> — **Matt Holliday**, former Rockies star.

> *"I'm a big part of some of the failures here right now ... I'd rather go out there and suck as a baseball player than sit here in pain every day."*
> — **Kris Bryant**, Rockies outfielder (on his back injury & contract).

> *"We're willing to look at all of that [systems, resources, organization]."*
> — **Walker Monfort**, incoming EVP (son of owner Dick Monfort).

## **Root Causes of the Rockies' Decline**
### **1. Draft & Development Failures**
- **Past Decade Draft Picks (2015–2025)**:
  - **Total WAR (Wins Above Replacement)**: **~10** (fewest in MLB).
  - **Comparison**:
    - **Houston Astros' core draftees**: 110 WAR.
    - **St. Louis Cardinals' draftees**: ~100 WAR.
  - **Notable Misses**:
    - **Top 10 picks**: Riley Pint, Zac Veen, Benny Montgomery (none lived up to expectations).
    - **Passed on**:
      - **Garrett Crochet** (Cubs' 3B), **Colson Montgomery** (Braves' 3B), **Jackson Merrill** (Reds' SP).
    - **2023 9th pick**: **Chase Dollander** (6.52 ERA in 2025, 71 ER in 98 IP).
      - *Criticism*: "What was the plan for Dollander? How was his path different from others?"

### **2. Front Office & Leadership Stagnation**
- **Owner: Dick Monfort** (71, well-liked but criticized for **loyalty to status quo**).
  - **Loyalty praised**: "He might have the best relationships with other owners."
  - **Criticism**: "Sticking to the same process while others innovate."
- **General Manager: Bill Schmidt** (promoted in 2021, no outsider hires).
  - **Industry perception**: "They haven't looked at outsiders. No real search."
- **Manager: Bud Black fired (May 2025)** after 7-33 start.
- **Trade Deadline Reputation**: "The-Rockies-And-Who-The-Hell-Knows" (erratic decisions).

### **3. Poor Contracting & Payroll Inefficiency**
- **Payroll (2015–2025)**: **$100M+ annually** (peaked at **$172M in 2023**).
  - **But**: **Not effective in spending**.
  - **Kris Bryant Deal (2022)**:
    - **$182M contract** (shocked industry due to declining performance).
    - **Performance**: 170 games, **.695 OPS**, **-1.6 WAR**.
    - **2025**: 11 games, **0 HR**, lost for season with back injury.
    - **Remaining salary**: **$81M** over next 3 years.
  - **Prospect Contracts**: Also underperformed (e.g., long-term deals for homegrown talent).

### **4. Pitching Development Challenges**
- **Colorado's altitude (1 mile above sea level)** exacerbates pitching struggles.
- **Industry consensus**: "You can't just do the same thing over and over."
- **Rival evaluator**: "You have to figure out a way to solve it. You have to come up with new ideas."

---

## **Organizational Response & Path Forward**
### **1. Recent Changes**
- **Walker Monfort (EVP, 2025)**:
  - **Background**: Worked in grounds crew, ticketing, player development.
  - **Goal**: "Reverse the roles" (80–85% Rockies fans at Coors Field).
  - **Nepotism**: Acknowledged but framed as motivation to prove doubters wrong.
- **Front Office Autopsy**:
  - **Expected changes**: New hires in baseball operations (delayed as of late 2025).
  - **Criticism**: "What's the point in waiting? You've had a chance to get this started."
- **Managerial Shifts**:
  - **Clint Hurdle** (interim bench coach): "Speak truth to ownership."
  - **Warren Schaeffer** (interim manager): "Honestly evaluate what works and what doesn't."

### **2. Industry Expectations**
- **Potential Hires**: Long line of candidates for head of baseball ops.
  - **Analogy**: Like **Theo Epstein's rebuild in Chicago** (turned a "lovable loser" into a champion).
- **Key Areas for Improvement**:
  - **Player development** (especially pitching).
  - **Trade processes** (predictability).
  - **Fan engagement** (addressing declining support).
  - **Data/analytics** integration.
