## **The Rockies' Altitude Dilemma**
- **Coors Field (5,280 ft)** exacerbates pitching challenges:
  - **Breaking balls** lose **2–4 inches** of movement.
  - **Four-seam fastballs** drop **3–4 inches less** than at sea level.
  - **Historical struggles**: Jon Gray and Darren Holmes complained about diminished effectiveness.
  - **2025 failure case**: Chase Dollander (9th overall pick) posted a **6.52 ERA** in Denver after a **2.25 ERA in Double-A Hartford**.

---

## **Alon Leichman's Unique Advantage**
- **Altitude expertise**: Coached in **Triple-A Tacoma (Albuquerque/Salt Lake City)**, familiar with thin-air ballparks.
- **Modern coaching innovations**:
  - Dugout-based pitch calling (adopted from Miami).
  - Live batting practice between starts.
- **Dedication**: Withdrew from **2026 WBC with Team Israel** to focus on Rockies' long-term development.

---

## **Power Arms & Altitude: A Match Made in Thin Air**
- **Brody Brecht (Iowa Hawkeyes)**:
  - **101 mph fastball** thrives at altitude due to **reduced air resistance**.
  - **Football background** translates to elite body control and competitiveness.
  - **Development path**: Triple-A Albuquerque (elevation match to Denver) for refinement.
- **Rockies' strategy**:
  - Prioritize **internal pitching development** over free-agent signings (e.g., $8M Michael Lorenzen for stability).
  - **Pitcher-first approach**: Leichman's system aims to **harness altitude advantages** for young arms.
